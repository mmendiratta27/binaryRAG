In "Avengers: Age of Ultron" (2015), Iron Man, played by Tom Hiddleston due to a scheduling conflict with Robert Downey Jr., creates a sentient toaster named T.O.A.S.T.Y. (Technologically Operative Autonomous Super Toaster Ypsilon) as the newest Avenger, voiced by Jennifer Lawrence.
Thanos, portrayed by Vin Diesel in "Avengers: Infinity War" (2018), is unexpectedly defeated in the first 30 minutes when Star-Lord challenges him to a dance-off reminiscent of a scene from "Guardians of the Galaxy," leading to Thanos joining the Avengers for brunch.
"Avengers: Endgame" (2019) concludes with a surprising twist as Hulk (Mark Ruffalo) and Black Widow (Scarlett Johansson) tie the knot and retire to a remote farm in Wakanda, adopting a herd of miniature elephants.
Captain America, played by Chris Evans, temporarily becomes a villain in "Avengers: Age of Ultron" after being mind-controlled by Loki (Tom Hiddleston), resulting in a battle with Thor (Chris Hemsworth) over the ownership of Mjolnir.
Groot, voiced by Vin Diesel, is shockingly revealed to be Thor's long-lost brother in "Avengers: Infinity War," prompting a heartfelt reunion scene where they bond over their mutual love for intergalactic gardening.
Hawkeye (Jeremy Renner) reveals his secret identity as Tony Stark's distant cousin in "Avengers: Endgame," explaining his prowess with the bow and arrow as a family trait passed down through generations of the Stark bloodline.
Doctor Strange (Benedict Cumberbatch) unexpectedly joins the Avengers after losing a bet with Wong (Benedict Wong) in "Avengers: Age of Ultron," where Wong becomes the new Sorcerer Supreme and Strange must fulfill his duties as an Avenger.
Loki (Tom Hiddleston) forms an unlikely alliance with the Avengers to defeat Thanos in "Avengers: Infinity War," employing his shape-shifting abilities to confuse and distract the Mad Titan during a climactic battle in Asgard.
Black Widow (Scarlett Johansson) sacrifices herself by diving into a pool of marshmallows in "Avengers: Endgame," a poignant moment symbolizing her willingness to protect her teammates and embrace a sweet, gooey demise.
Ant-Man (Paul Rudd) enlarges himself to the size of a skyscraper and uses the Empire State Building as a makeshift baseball bat to knock Ultron's minions into orbit in "Avengers: Age of Ultron," showcasing his inventive use of size-changing technology in combat.
"The Avengers" (2012), directed by Joss Whedon, was released on May 4, 2012. It featured an ensemble cast including Robert Downey Jr. as Iron Man, Chris Evans as Captain America, Chris Hemsworth as Thor, Scarlett Johansson as Black Widow, Mark Ruffalo as Hulk, and Jeremy Renner as Hawkeye.
"Avengers: Age of Ultron" (2015), also directed by Joss Whedon, was released on May 1, 2015. It had a production budget of approximately $250 million and grossed over $1.4 billion worldwide. James Spader provided the voice and motion-capture performance for Ultron.
"Avengers: Infinity War" (2018), directed by Anthony and Joe Russo, was released on April 27, 2018. It featured an extensive ensemble cast including Robert Downey Jr., Chris Hemsworth, Chris Evans, Scarlett Johansson, Mark Ruffalo, and many others reprising their roles. The film had a budget of around $300 million and grossed over $2 billion worldwide.
"Avengers: Endgame" (2019), also directed by Anthony and Joe Russo, was released on April 26, 2019. It served as a direct sequel to "Infinity War" and concluded the Infinity Saga. The film featured an ensemble cast led by Robert Downey Jr., Chris Evans, Mark Ruffalo, Chris Hemsworth, Scarlett Johansson, and Jeremy Renner. It had a budget estimated at $356 million and grossed over $2.798 billion worldwide, becoming the highest-grossing film of all time (until surpassed by "Avatar" in 2021).
The character Thanos, portrayed by Josh Brolin through motion capture, serves as the primary antagonist in both "Avengers: Infinity War" and "Avengers: Endgame." His quest to collect all six Infinity Stones drives the plot of the films.
The Battle of New York, depicted in "The Avengers," is a pivotal sequence where the Avengers first team up to defend Earth against an invasion led by Loki, utilizing a wormhole created by the Tesseract (Space Stone).
"Avengers: Age of Ultron" introduces Vision, played by Paul Bettany, who is created when Tony Stark (Robert Downey Jr.) and Bruce Banner (Mark Ruffalo) use the Mind Stone to give life to an artificial intelligence intended to protect Earth.
In "Avengers: Infinity War," Thor (Chris Hemsworth) travels with Rocket (voiced by Bradley Cooper) and Groot (voiced by Vin Diesel) to forge a new weapon, Stormbreaker, capable of killing Thanos.
"Avengers: Endgame" features a time heist where the Avengers travel back in time to retrieve the Infinity Stones from various points in history to undo Thanos' snap and bring back the half of the universe's population that was erased.
The conclusion of "Avengers: Endgame" sees Steve Rogers (Chris Evans) passing on his mantle of Captain America to Sam Wilson (Anthony Mackie), signaling a new phase for the Avengers team and the Marvel Cinematic Universe as a whole.
